Overview

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action models are powerful for generalizable visuomotor control, but most existing VLAs still operate at the image level and rely heavily on textual prompts. PixelVLA addresses both limitations by introducing pixel-level reasoning together with multimodal prompting using text and visual inputs.

The framework combines a visual prompt-aware encoder, a multiscale pixel-aware encoder, and a continuous action decoder, and is trained with a new visuomotor instruction tuning recipe. To support this, the paper also proposes Pixel-160K, a large-scale robot dataset with pixel-level annotations, visual prompts, and action supervision. Across three standard benchmarks and two VLA model variants, PixelVLA reports consistent gains while using only a small fraction of OpenVLA's pretraining cost.

01

Visual prompt-aware encoder

Accepts points, lines, boxes, regions, and masks, so the policy can respond to richer forms of human guidance than text alone.

02

Multiscale pixel-aware encoder

Injects fine-grained pixel information into token representations, improving spatial precision and object-level reasoning for manipulation.

03

Continuous action decoder

Predicts continuous robot actions directly, preserving action detail that can be lost with coarse token discretization.

Why these results matter

  • Pixel-level understanding improves spatial precision in manipulation tasks.
  • Visual prompting broadens the interaction interface beyond pure language.
  • The method transfers across multiple robot platforms and benchmark styles.
  • The gains come with relatively modest extra training cost.
